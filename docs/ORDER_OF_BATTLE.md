## NOTES FOR WARGAME PURPOSES

1. **Soviet mechanised corps** are the key mobile strike forces on the Soviet side but suffered from poor radio communications, inadequate logistics, and mixed tank quality. Many corps had T-26 and BT-series tanks; only a fraction had the newer T-34 and KV-1/KV-2.

2. **German Panzer Groups** are the decisive offensive instrument; their speed and coordination with Luftwaffe close air support drove encirclement battles at Białystok–Minsk, Uman, and Kiev.

3. **Axis satellite forces** (Romanian, Hungarian, Finnish) are generally lower quality than German formations and are best represented at reduced combat values.

4. **Soviet order of battle changed rapidly** in the first weeks as armies were destroyed, reformed, and new formations raised. The above reflects the June 22 snapshot only.

5. **Finnish forces** operated semi-independently under Mannerheim and did not cross the 1940 armistice line until late June–early July, after German operations had already begun.
